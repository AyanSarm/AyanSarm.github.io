The second season of Adventure Time premiered on Cartoon Network from October 11, 2010 to May 9, 2011 (US), and contains a total of twenty six 11-minute episodes. The first episode of the second season of Adventure Time, "It Came from the Nightosphere", premiered on Cartoon Network on Monday October 11, 2010 at 8:00 pm with 2 million US viewers. The episode that had the highest number of US viewers in season 2 was "Loyalty to the King.

Major characters

Finn

Finn is a teenage human, resembling one in most of his appearances throughout the series. He was roughly five feet at the show's debut, but sometime between "The Cooler" and "The Pajama War," he underwent a growth spurt and is currently nearly as tall as Princess Bubblegum.

Marceline

Marceline is a fun-loving and playful 1,000-year-old vampire queen. Unlike a traditional vampire, Marceline does not need to drink blood to survive; rather, she consumes the color red, or other similar tints. Marceline is also an avid musician who plays an electric bass guitar, which she crafted from her family's heirloom battle-axe.

Hunson Abadeer

Hunson Abadeer is Marceline's father and the ruler of the Nightosphere. He is a powerful, deathless demon who is full of chaotic evil. He also enjoys consuming the souls of others, but does not spend much time out of the Nightosphere.

Minor characters

Jake

Jake is a magical dog and Finn's constant companion, best friend, and adoptive brother. Jake has Stretchy Powers, which allow him to stretch and manipulate his body, coming in handy on innumerable occasions throughout his and Finn's adventures.

Gunter

Gunter is the penguin that most commonly accompanies the Ice King. The Ice King uses Gunter as his personal servant, whereas the other penguins working for the Ice King are essentially slaves.[2] In reality, Gunter is the primordial cosmic entity known as the Orgalorg and feared as the Breaker of Worlds.

Ice king

Ice king is originally depicted as a two-dimensional, humorously over-the-top villain trying to kidnap Princess Bubblegum and various other Princesses on a regular basis, the character's personality was subsequently expanded, revealing him to be a kind-hearted but misunderstood old man with completely twisted senses of morality and social behavior, suffering from loneliness and memory loss.
